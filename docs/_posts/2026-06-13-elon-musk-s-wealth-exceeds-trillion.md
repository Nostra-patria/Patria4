Elon Musk became the world's first trillionaire on Friday, June 12, 2026, following SpaceX's initial public offering on the Nasdaq. His net worth is estimated between $1.05 trillion and $1.26 trillion in paper assets, driven by a SpaceX market valuation of approximately $2.1 trillion and his holdings in Tesla.

SpaceX shares began trading on the Nasdaq at $150 per share, valuing the company at approximately $2.1 trillion. Forbes estimates Musk's total net worth at $1.1 trillion.

The milestone drew immediate political reaction in the United States. Senator Elizabeth Warren (D-Mass) called for a wealth tax and increased SEC due diligence, stating that Musk "just became the world's first trillionaire" while typical Americans struggle.

Financial analysts note that the trillion-dollar figure represents "paper wealth" rather than liquid assets. The valuation reflects the market's assessment of SpaceX's dominance in satellite internet via Starlink and its position as the primary provider of space launch services.

Reports from Times Now note that this fortune exceeds the annual GDP of several sovereign nations, while Reuters reports that critics have described this level of influence as "oligarch-like power" due to Musk's control over global infrastructure in AI, robotics, and orbital transport.
