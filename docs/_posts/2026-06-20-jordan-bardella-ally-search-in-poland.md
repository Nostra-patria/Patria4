Jordan Bardella, president of France's National Rally, concluded a two-day diplomatic tour of Poland on June 19, 2026, meeting with President Karol Nawrocki and right-wing leaders to build a pan-European alliance. The visit focused on coordinating opposition to Ukraine's EU accession and deepening bilateral military cooperation as Bardella positions himself as a statesman-in-waiting for the 2027 French presidential election.

The visit featured a high-level meeting with Polish President Karol Nawrocki, where both leaders identified "clear convergences of view" regarding migration, security, and the role of EU bureaucracy. Nawrocki described the session as a "very good conversation about the future of Europe, security, and the role of sovereign states in the European community." Bardella and Nawrocki discussed a vision for a "Europe of sovereign nations" that operates within the existing Union framework but prioritizes national interests over centralized directives. President Nawrocki, a conservative historian who won the presidency with 50.89% of the vote, used his platform to contrast this vision with the current administration, accusing the Tusk government of "weakening Poland's position with Washington."

A central pillar of the coordination is a shared opposition to Ukraine's accession to the European Union. In talks with Krzysztof Bosak, leader of the far-right Konfederacja coalition, Bardella agreed to jointly oppose the admission of Ukraine, citing a failure to meet EU standards and unfair economic competition. Bosak stated that the two movements share a common vision, specifically citing "opposition to immigration, blocking the admission of Ukraine to the EU, [and the] abandonment of suicidal climate policy." Bosak has explicitly urged Poland to block Ukraine's entry until legal and historical issues, specifically exhumations, are resolved. This stance aligns with a period of high tension between Warsaw and Kyiv, during which President Nawrocki stripped President Zelenskyy of Poland's highest state honour over disputes regarding the Ukrainian Insurgent Army.

Security cooperation also featured prominently on the agenda. Bardella announced that France and Poland would "deepen their cooperation in military matters," citing the strategic necessity of doing so amid the risk of "American disengagement" in Europe. To underscore this focus on security, Bardella visited the Belarusian border on June 19 to observe current security conditions and border management.

The tour included institutional outreach to the Law and Justice (PiS) party, the main opposition in Poland. On June 18, Bardella arrived in Warsaw, met with Krzysztof Bosak, and toured the Sejm. He later held meetings with PiS leadership to establish a political base for a broader right-wing alliance. Bardella also engaged in symbolic gestures of Polish national identity, visiting the memorial to the heroes of the Warsaw Ghetto Uprising and laying flowers at the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier.

Bardella framed these diplomatic efforts as a prerequisite for the 2027 French presidential election, stating his goal is to "change everything without destroying anything" within the EU. He told reporters that if his movement and its Polish allies achieve electoral victories, they will have the opportunity to "redirect the way the European Union operates" and put the European Commission "back at the service of the nations."

This diplomatic tour of Poland is part of a broader strategic effort by Bardella to consolidate a right-wing axis across Europe. The visit follows similar trips to Italy on April 18, Portugal on April 22, and Belgium on June 11, where he met with Vlaams Belang. These engagements were conducted in his capacity as chair of the "Patriotes pour l'Europe" group in the European Parliament and serve as the foundation for his 2027 presidential bid, aiming to establish a network of nationalist allies capable of reshaping European Union governance from within.
